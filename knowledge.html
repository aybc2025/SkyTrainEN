What is SkyTrain?

SkyTrain is a fast city train that runs by itself without a driver on separated tracks ‚Äì inside tunnels or on high bridges. The computer keeps distance, stops at stations precisely, and alerts about problems.

üí°
The system started in December 1985 and has been expanding ever since. Many tracks are elevated ‚Äì that's why it's called "SkyTrain".

General info summarized from TransLink/BCRTC sources.

Golden Rules for Young Riders

  • Stand behind the yellow line.
  • Let passengers exit first.
  • Hold the handrail on escalators, and keep quiet for announcements.
  • Look for stations together on the map and mark them in a "treasure hunt" game.

The Short Story

Vancouver wanted fast, safe and clean transportation. In 1985 the system opened, and in 1986 during Expo 86 it got huge publicity. Since then, lines, stations and bridges have been added (including the SkyBridge over the Fraser River), and in 2009 the Canada Line opened to the airport.

How does the train "drive itself"?

  • Smart computers and advanced signaling control spacing and speed.
  • Sensors check where trains are located.
  • Control center with people supervising 24/7 and helping in emergencies.
  • Everything runs on electricity ‚Äì clean and relatively quiet.

Stations & Accessibility

  • Elevators and ramps at most stations.
  • Colored signage by lines and voice announcements.
  • Compass smart card for entry and exit gates.

Mark I ‚Äì The Veteran

  • In service since: 1985.
  • What does it look like? Short cars, usually sitting along the sides.
  • How many cars? Usually 6 cars per train ("married pairs").
  • Where does it run? Expo/Millennium lines (mainly Expo).
  • Facts: car ‚âà ~12.7m; two doors per side; upgrade/refresh project done in previous decade.

Mark II ‚Äì The Long "Married Pair"

  • In service since: Early 2000s (various deliveries).
  • What does it look like? Two units connected ("married pair") with passage in middle.
  • How many cars? Usually 4 cars (two pairs connected).
  • Where does it run? Expo and Millennium.
  • Facts: connected pair ‚âà 33.4m; 3 doors per side; built by Bombardier.

Mark III ‚Äì Modern with Big Windows

  • In service since: 2016 (deliveries until ~2020).
  • What does it look like? Modern design, large spaces and convenient handholds.
  • How many cars? Usually 4 cars.
  • Where does it run? Mainly Expo Line.
  • Facts: similar length to Mark II; updated propulsion/control systems.

Mark V ‚Äì The Newest üöÄ

  • In service since: 2025 (entered service on Expo Line).
  • What does it look like? Long train with continuous walkways throughout, new design and improved AC.
  • How many cars? 5 fixed cars per train.
  • Approximate capacity: Up to ~672 passengers per train (about 25% higher than 4-car Mark III).
  • What for? To increase capacity and prepare for Surrey‚ÄìLangley extension.

Canada Line ‚Äì Hyundai Rotem Trains (EMU)

  • In service since: 2009 (second delivery 2019).
  • What does it look like? Stainless steel body, two cars connected (can link to 4).
  • How many cars? 2 cars per set (sometimes linked to pairs = 4).
  • Set length: ‚âà ~41m; width: ‚âà 3.0m.
